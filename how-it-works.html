How it Works

Turning imperfect produce into perfect impact — reducing waste, supporting farmers, and supplying fresh food to schools through local council partnerships.

Sourced from farms

Farmers send us produce that doesn’t meet supermarket cosmetic standards. These misshapen, slightly blemished or “imperfect” fruit and vegetables are still completely fresh and nutritious.

Quality checked

Every batch is carefully inspected to ensure it meets food safety standards. Only produce that is safe, fresh, and high-quality moves forward in the supply chain.

Collected & sorted

We collect produce directly from farms and sort it into distribution streams, prioritising schools, community kitchens, and local food programmes.

Council partnerships

We work with local councils to coordinate demand and logistics, ensuring food reaches communities efficiently and fairly.

Delivered to schools

Fresh produce is delivered to schools across the region, supporting healthy meals, reducing costs, and improving access to nutrition.

Less Waste, More Impact

Instead of being discarded, perfectly edible produce feeds people — reducing waste, emissions, and supporting a fairer food system.

The Impact

By redirecting imperfect produce into public food systems, we help reduce agricultural waste at scale while improving access to fresh food in education settings.
