Supporting Schools Through Waste Reduction

Farmerli partners with local councils to redirect surplus and imperfect produce from farms into schools, community kitchens, and public food programmes — reducing waste while improving access to fresh ingredients.

Produce Is Sourced From Local Farms

Farmerli works directly with regional growers to recover fruit and vegetables that may not meet supermarket cosmetic standards but remain completely fresh, safe, and nutritious.

This includes misshapen produce, irregular sizing, surface blemishes, and seasonal surplus that would otherwise risk being wasted despite being fully edible.

Farmerli Coordinates Collection & Logistics

Produce is collected from participating farms through coordinated regional logistics routes designed to minimise transport inefficiencies and environmental impact.

Farmerli handles sorting, quality assurance, packaging, and delivery planning to create a reliable and scalable supply chain for councils and public sector food programmes.

Councils Coordinate Local Demand

Local councils work with Farmerli to identify schools, meal providers, and community food services that can benefit from regular fresh produce supply.

Delivery schedules and volumes can be adapted based on seasonal availability, programme requirements, and local demand levels.

Fresh Produce Reaches Schools & Communities

Fresh fruit and vegetables are delivered directly into schools, educational catering systems, and local community programmes to improve access to nutritious ingredients.

This helps schools introduce more fresh produce into meals while reducing procurement pressure and supporting local agricultural networks.

Lower Waste, Lower Emissions

By redirecting edible produce away from landfill and into public food systems, councils can reduce food waste while contributing toward sustainability and emissions reduction targets.

Localised sourcing and optimised delivery routes also reduce unnecessary transport and supply chain waste.

6. Building Long-Term Community Food Networks

Farmerli partnerships help create more resilient local food systems by connecting farms, councils, schools, and communities through sustainable regional supply networks.

Over time, these partnerships help strengthen food accessibility, support regional agriculture, and reduce avoidable waste across the wider food economy.

Why Councils Partner With Farmerli

Farmerli helps local authorities reduce waste, improve access to fresh food, and support regional farms through practical, scalable produce redistribution programmes.
